How it works
  • Each generation, cells live or die based on their neighbors (the 8 surrounding cells).
  • Grass survives with 2-3 neighbors. Spreads where 3 neighbors exist. Counts grass and flowers.
  • Flowers survive with 2-4 neighbors, only counting flowers. Age and die after 30 gens.
  • Bees thrive near flowers. They pollinate: flowers near bees spread more easily. Die after 20 gens.
  • Rabbits live near grass and other rabbits. They eat grass: grass next to 2+ rabbits dies. Die after 25 gens.
